Tools & Features

  • Reproducibility. Powerful tools such as the macro recorder and personal update sites help you develop and share reproducible analysis workflows.
  • Open source. Understanding the process of producing scientific results is equally important as the results themselves. To that end, the ImageJ application and its source code will always be freely available.
  • Interoperability. ImageJ is not an island. Use the tools that best fit the problem, including KNIME, ITK, MATLAB, and a multitude of scripting languages.

Join the Community

  • The mailing lists will connect you with thousands of other ImageJ users and developers. Ask and answer questions here and become part of the community that has driven ImageJ's success.
  • The ImageJ team uses GitHub issues for bug reports, technical suggestions and feature requests.
  • If you need more immediate help, the FAQ and chat channels are a great starting point.

ImageJ is developed by Curtis Rueden, Mark Hiner and the ImageJ development team at the Laboratory for Optical and Computational Instrumentation (LOCI) at the University of Wisconsin-Madison.
