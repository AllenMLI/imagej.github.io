'''Particle Tracker''' is a 2D and 3D feature point-tracking tool for the automated detection and tracking of particle trajectories as recorded by video imaging in cell biology. It is part of the [http://mosaic.mpi-cbg.de/?q=downloads/imageJ MosaicSuite], which also offers image segmentation, interaction analysis, and much more. The best way to install it is via the MOSAIC Fiji Update site, as described on the [http://mosaic.mpi-cbg.de/?q=downloads/imageJ MOSAIC web page].
The algorithm is described in Sbalzarini and Koumoutsakos (2005<ref>I. F. Sbalzarini and P. Koumoutsakos. [http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/j.jsb.2005.06.002 Feature point tracking and trajectory analysis for video imaging in cell biology], J. Struct. Biol., 151(2):182-195, 2005</ref>).
A recent comparative study of many particle tracking tools has named this tool as one of the top choices: Chenouard et al. (2014<ref>N. Chenouard et al. [http://dx.doi.org/doi:10.1038/nMeth.2808 Objective comparison of particle tracking methods], Nature Methods, 11(3):281-289, 2014</ref>).

Documentation is available as [http://mosaic.mpi-cbg.de/ParticleTracker/tutorial.html html] and [http://mosaic.mpi-cbg.de/ParticleTracker/doc/ParticleTrackerUserManual.pdf pdf].
